The Unicorn

The unicorn in the current Western myth is shaped like a white horse, with a spiral angle on its forehead, representing nobility, pride and purity. Some stories are described as having wings, and even unicorns are black. The prototype of the Western Unicorn has been recognized as the Indian rhinoceros.

The Imperial Palace

The Imperial Palace

The Imperial Palace is the imperial palace of Ming and Qing Dynasties, formerly known as the Forbidden City, located in the center of Beijing's central axis. The Palace Museum in Beijing is centered on the three main halls, covering an area of 720000 square meters and a construction area of about 150000 square meters. There are more than 70 palaces and more than 9000 houses.

The Whale

The Whale

Cetaceans are the most suitable modern mammals to live in water. In the process of transformation from terrestrial to aquatic, cetaceans have evolved into fish like shapes. Meanwhile, body hair degenerates (some cetaceans still have a small amount of setae on the snout of their newborn larvae, most of them fade out during their growth, and a small number of species remain for life). Sweat glands and sebaceous glands also degenerate with the final complete aquatic life of cetaceans.
